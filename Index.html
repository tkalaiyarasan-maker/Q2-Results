Strategy, Outlook & Key Takeaways

This final section outlines L&T's forward-looking strategy, the macroeconomic context, and management's guidance. It concludes with a summary of the key strengths and potential risks identified in the report, providing a balanced view of the company's position.

Strategic Updates (Lakshya 2026)

1. Divestment Move

In-principle agreement to divest stake in L&T Metro Rail (Hyderabad), aligning with goal to exit non-core concession assets.

2. Tech & Digital Expansion

Investing in semiconductors, data centres, and clean energy. Targeting synergy between LTIMindtree and LTTS.

3. Financial Services Pivot

L&T Finance is now ~98% retail-focussed (housing, farm, vehicle loans), using AI for credit evaluation to improve margins.

4. Clean Energy Push

Expanding capacity in renewables, electrolyser manufacturing, and carbon capture projects.

Management Guidance & Macro Outlook

“We continue to witness strong capex momentum across India and the Middle East, and remain optimistic about order prospects.”
— S. N. Subrahmanyan, CMD, L&T

FY26 Guidance

  • Revenue Growth: Low to mid-teens
  • Margin Range: 9.8% – 10.3%
  • Order Inflow: Expected to remain robust
  • Focus: Cash conversion, digital growth

Macroeconomic Outlook

  • India: GDP projected at 6.5-7.0%
  • Global: Growth slowdown (~3%)
  • Middle East: Positive momentum, large EPC spending

Key Takeaways

✅ Strengths

  • Record order inflow, diversified order book
  • Strong overseas performance
  • Positive cash flow turnaround
  • Reduced debt-equity ratio & strong liquidity

⚠️ Risks

  • Execution delays (weather, regulatory)
  • Margin pressure in Energy & IT
  • Global macro & geopolitical risks
